The Economics of Losing

March 2026

At some point, losing stops being a failure of execution.

It becomes something else—something quieter, more intentional, and far more profitable.

Teams don’t just lose because they’re bad. They lose because losing, under the right conditions, is efficient.

Draft position. Payroll flexibility. Reset narratives. The illusion of a future that always feels one year away.

Fans call it rebuilding. Front offices call it optionality.
